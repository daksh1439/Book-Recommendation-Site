LETS HAVE A LOOK AT THE ...

POPULAR BOOKS
  OF 2024

2024 has been a year of bookish adventures! From heart-pounding thrillers to heartwarming romances, there's been something for every reader. We and many other readers have been hooked on certain books this year, lost in their pages, and eagerly awaiting the next chapter. With so many amazing stories to choose from, it's no wonder we're already planning our 2025 reading list. So, grab your favorite beverage, cozy up with a good book, and get ready for another year of literary bliss!

Lets have a look at BOOK NOOK's most popular books of the year:-

Fiction:
1.

The Midnight Library by Matt Haig
A reflective and heartwarming novel, The Midnight Library explores the concept of life's choices and regrets through the lens of Nora Seed, who finds herself in a library between life and death. Each book in this library represents a different life she could have lived, offering her the chance to reconsider her past and make different decisions. This exploration of regret, fulfillment, and personal growth has resonated deeply with readers, making it a significant work in 2024

The books of 2024 have offered readers a diverse and captivating range of stories, blending heartwarming fiction with thought-provoking non-fiction.

2.

Pachinko by Min Jin Lee
Min Jin Lee’s Pachinko is a sweeping multi-generational epic that traces the lives of a Korean family in Japan, navigating the complex intersection of identity, immigration, and perseverance. The novel explores themes of love, hardship, and the pursuit of belonging, offering a rich narrative that spans decades. The story’s emotional depth and its portrayal of cultural identity have made it a lasting favorite and essential reading in 2024​

3.

The Vanishing Half by Brit Bennett
The Vanishing Half follows the lives of twin sisters, one of whom lives as a Black woman while the other passes as white. This compelling narrative delves into themes of race, identity, and family, examining how different choices affect the trajectory of each sister’s life. The book’s exploration of identity and the societal forces that shape us has made it one of the most talked-about novels in recent years

4.

"The Husbands" by Holly Gramazio
This romantic comedy with a surreal twist follows Lauren, a thirty-something woman who discovers that she has mysteriously acquired a husband upon returning home one evening. As the plot unfolds, Lauren realizes that she can send each husband into the attic to be replaced by another. With each new husband comes a new life and career, leading to hilarious and sometimes absurd situations. The novel cleverly satirizes modern dating, presenting a smart, funny, and unique narrative. It's a perfect read for those looking for an enjoyable escape​

5.

"Intermezzo" by Sally Rooney
In Intermezzo, Sally Rooney returns with her signature style, offering a deep dive into the complexities of human relationships. The novel explores the lives of two main characters as they navigate love, family, and self-discovery. Rooney's strength lies in her ability to dissect emotions and relationships, creating a nuanced portrayal of intimacy and personal growth. Fans of Normal People and Conversations with Friends will find much to love in this book, which is already garnering praise for its layered character development​

Time For Some Non Fiction...

POPULAR BOOKS
     OF 2024

1.

"Sapiens: A Brief History of Humankind" by Yuval Noah Harari
Sapiens delves into the history of humankind, examining how Homo sapiens came to dominate the Earth. Harari explores the cognitive revolution, the agricultural revolution, and the scientific revolution, offering insights into how these pivotal changes shaped the world we live in today. It’s a thought-provoking exploration of humanity's past and its future.


2.

"The Body Keeps the Score" by Bessel van der Kolk
This book takes a deep dive into the effects of trauma on the body and mind. Dr. van der Kolk explores the ways trauma impacts individuals, drawing on cutting-edge research in neuroscience and psychology. It also offers new perspectives on healing, emphasizing the importance of mind-body connections in recovery.

The books of 2024 have offered readers a diverse and captivating range of stories, blending heartwarming fiction with thought-provoking non-fiction

3.

"Educated" by Tara Westover
Tara Westover's memoir tells the extraordinary story of her upbringing in a strict, survivalist family in rural Idaho. With no formal schooling, she eventually escapes to pursue education, earning a PhD from the University of Cambridge. Her journey of self-discovery and transformation is both inspiring and heartbreaking.

4.

"King: A Life" by Jonathan Eig
This biography of Martin Luther King Jr. offers a nuanced portrait of the civil rights icon, exploring his life beyond the public persona. Eig's work dives deep into King's personal and political struggles, offering fresh insights into his legacy


5.

"The House of Hidden Meanings" by RuPaul
In this memoir, the drag superstar shares his personal journey from a difficult childhood to becoming a global icon of self-expression and acceptance. It's a fascinating story of resilience and transformation
